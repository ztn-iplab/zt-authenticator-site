How it works

From enrollment to approval, the workflow is intentionally human.

01

Scan secure enrollment QR

QR encodes domain, enrollment token, and policy hints.

02

Generate device keypair

Keys are stored in the OS keystore and never exported.

03

Approve login requests

Device proof + TOTP used to finalize authentication.

04

Sync policy feedback

Trust engine receives device response and risk context.

Common questions

What teams ask before rolling out ZT-Authenticator.

Is ZT-Authenticator tied to ZT-IAM?

No. It integrates tightly with ZT-IAM but supports custom enrollment payloads for any IAM platform.

How do RPs stay consistent?

Each enrollment is bound to a relying party domain so login approvals and policy checks remain scoped to the correct service.

Does it work offline?

Yes. TOTP generation is offline. Device approvals sync once the connection resumes.

How do we handle lost devices?

Admins can revoke device keys and require re-enrollment without leaking seeds.

Can users choose MFA types?

Yes. Policies can enable TOTP, WebAuthn, or both with device approval when required.
